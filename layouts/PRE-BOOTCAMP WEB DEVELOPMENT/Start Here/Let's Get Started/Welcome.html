    Welcome

    Congratulations, future developer! Only a small percentage of applicants find themselves in the Digital Academy, and we're glad you're here. 

    Your journey begins today: the day you embark on the Pre-Bootcamp work.

    The Pre-Bootcamp work is intended to ensure you and all your cohort-mates enter the Digital Academy with the same level of basic knowledge. Working through this content, you will also experience the Digital Academy-way of learning and get insight into what to expect of yourself, the instructors, and the bootcamp as a whole. It includes introductory information around programming basics, algorithmic reasoning, web development, computer technical skills, and learning aptitudes - all to set you up for success in the program. 

    Estimated Time: 15 - 30 hrs

    Incoming students typically take 15 - 30 hours to complete the Pre-Bootcamp required materials. If you have programming experience, it will be closer to 15 hours. No programming experience? Then it's closer to 30 hours or above. MAKE SURE TO CHECK OUT THE REQUIREMENTS PAGE so you know what you are expected to have completed prior. 

    A few tips about using this Learn platform:

    • Terminology: Stacks / Courses / Chapters / Pages: That's the hierarchy of the Learn platform. Right now, you are in the Pre-Bootcamp Stack / Getting Started Course / Let's Get Started Chapter / Welcome page. This will not be on the test, it's just good to know.
    • Code Challenges: These appear as Purple in the left navigation bar and are the activities you will be doing to practice your skills. A few special notes about these: there are a lot of these and that's intentional. We want you to practice often! This means instructors can't feasibly look at every single code challenge you do. However, you can still get feedback. If you want feedback on these elements, first, get your peers to look at it. Then the TA. There are a lot of opportunities to practice what you're learning, so do all of them that you can. Remember: it's not "getting it right," it's about the process of taking on the challenge.    

    Questions? Contact your local Assistant Director of Admissions.
