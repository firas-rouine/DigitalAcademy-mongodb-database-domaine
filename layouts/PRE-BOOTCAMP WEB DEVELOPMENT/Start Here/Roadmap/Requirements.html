    Requirements

    Code Challenges

    You must complete 70% of all required code challenges, sometimes referred to as assignments. This is the bare minimum to eek by. Although some challenges are marked "optional", we really want students to complete all of them by the end of the bootcamp. Sometimes this means doing the required challenges and then later returning to the optionals. Similar to how video games have a variety of playable content you can return to play through again, return to stacks and do the optional assignments if you can't complete them within the stack time frame.  

    Belt Exams

    Each stack you go through will build upon the concepts you learned in the previous stack. Therefore, it is imperative that you build your foundation with your first stack before progressing to the future stacks. We test your proficiency at the end of each stack with a belt exam. We require that you pass your Web Fundamentals and Python belt exams (you earn either a red belt or a black belt) in order to move on to your next stack. If you do not pass the Web Fundamentals or Python belt exams, we will ask you to repeat the corresponding stack. 

    Honor code

    Most importantly, the belt exams are a way for you to determine whether you’ve gained the skills being offered by each stack. The belt exams are difficult, and they are designed to be so. When you pass them, you know without a doubt that you are a full stack developer. You joined the bootcamp to become a self-sufficient developer, and the belt exam is an opportunity to give yourself insight into your progress on that journey. We ask all our students to hold to an honor code and to turn in only original work for the exams. Any submitted code which is found to be plagiarized will result in expulsion from the bootcamp.

    Attendance

    We take attendance. We expect all current students to be present as outlined in the Catalog. If you are unable to attend for any reason, you must inform your instructors so they may plan accordingly. To be eligible for graduation, you must meet the attendance requirements as outlined in Catalog.

    Practice, practice, practice

    Spend as much time as possible every day studying the material. Study time depends on the your programming experience. Since most of our students are new to web development, it is helpful to spend as many hours as you can per day on studying. This often requires you to put in some hours on the weekend.

    Working Together (*pair programming)

    We encourage our students to work with each other. We notice that students learn and absorb information better when they get in groups of two and three. By working together, students get to discuss the content and explain to each other different aspects of the topics. There is a frequent exchange of information which is critical to learning. Often we’ll ask you to pair program, which means two people working together on one computer. While one person types, the other is giving directions, and the two switch roles regularly. Other times, we’ll ask a group to work together, but have everyone write the code on his or her own machine. Regardless, you'll still need to be able to code on your own. So, obviously, exams are not the time to use pair programming.

    Attitude

    We emphasize the importance of a good attitude and hold a zero tolerance policy for bad attitudes or disrespect. We believe that a great attitude makes a massive difference in the classroom feel. Things will get difficult, but remember to remain humble. Accept that you will make mistakes, and appreciate that they are learning opportunities. With a good attitude and hard work, you'll be able to accomplish a lot more!

    Passion

    This is just as important as attitude. If you are passionate about development you will definitely have a great time! Remember why you are here when times get hard and when the workload gets heavy. Your passion for learning and improving will help you through the program. The casual 5 minute break helps a lot as well. If you find yourself frustrated, take a quick break away from the computer. Take a short walk and come back with a clear mind.

    Focus

    We all have busy lives. During the bootcamp, you must put other aspects of your life on hold for its duration. It's crucial to reduce the distractions while going through the program and to create a good daily rhythm for yourself to study and focus. Even the most passionate and positive students need to stay focused on being successful. Don't underestimate the power of focus and a great study environment.
